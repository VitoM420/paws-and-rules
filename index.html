The Core Problems

Why the System Is Broken

Estate and body corporate rules are often vague, inconsistently enforced, and ignore both the rights of residents and the welfare of animals.

01
Blanket Bans Without Due Process

Many estates enforce total pet bans regardless of animal type, size, or behaviour history. Residents have no opportunity to demonstrate responsibility before being penalised.

02
Retroactive Rule Changes

Long-term residents who moved in with pets find rules changed mid-tenure by new trustees or body corporates, with little warning and even less recourse.

03
No Exceptions for Vulnerable Groups

Elderly residents, people with disabilities, and those relying on emotional support animals are routinely denied accommodation, despite legal protections that should apply.

04
Opaque Decision-Making

Applications for pet permission are rejected without reasons. Trustees operate without accountability. Appeals processes are either non-existent or stacked against the resident.

05
Disproportionate Fines & Intimidation

Pet owners face repeated inspections, threatening letters, and escalating levies. The financial and psychological toll is immense — often forcing compliance through fear.

06
Animal Welfare Neglected

When families are forced to surrender pets, animals end up in overcrowded shelters with low adoption rates. The human housing crisis creates a parallel animal welfare crisis.

What We're Fighting For

Fair Rules for Pet Owners

We're not asking for chaos — we're asking for fairness, transparency, and respect for the human-animal bond.

📋
Reasonable Pet Policies

Estates must adopt clear, size-and-breed-specific guidelines rather than blanket bans. Good tenants with well-behaved pets deserve a fair assessment process.

🔔
Adequate Notice Periods

Any change to pet rules must include a minimum 6-month transition period for existing residents — giving families time to adapt without trauma.

♿
ESA & Service Animal Exemptions

Emotional support and certified service animals must be explicitly exempted from pet bans, in line with disability rights legislation.

⚖️
Independent Appeals Process

Every rejected application deserves a written reason and access to an independent ombudsman — not just the same committee that made the initial decision.

🏛️
Legislative Reform

We call on government to amend the Sectional Titles Act to establish minimum standards for pet ownership rights in community-living environments.

🤝
Community Mediation

Disputes between trustees and pet owners should be resolved through neutral mediation before any eviction process can begin. Dialogue over displacement.
